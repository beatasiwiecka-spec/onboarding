1. Loyalty Program Tiers — Platinum / Gold / Silver

▼

The current data-derived tier segmentation reveals strong concentration at the Silver level and high-value behavior among Platinum members. Tier design should reflect actual spend thresholds and incentivize upward mobility.

⚡ Top 6% of members (Platinum) drive 27.79% of revenue with RPM 4x the average ($597 vs $129). 89.65% of Platinum members are repeat buyers — this tier has the highest loyalty signal.
📊 Gold tier cutoff sits at P75 ($154.98 spend) and Platinum at P94 ($364.95). These thresholds should anchor tier entry points in the program design.

REC 1

Anchor Tiers to Data-Derived Spend Thresholds

Set Silver entry at $0, Gold at $155 (P75), Platinum at $365 (P94) cumulative spend. Communicate progress bars clearly to drive aspiration.

Impact: High
REC 2

Platinum Exclusive Perks — Protect & Reward

Early boarding, dedicated support line, bonus points on Coach Canada routes. Focus on the 89.65% repeat Platinum buyers — they are the most loyal base.

Impact: High
REC 3

Gold → Platinum Upgrade Campaign

Gold members (44K) are close to Platinum. A "spend $X more to unlock Platinum" dynamic progress nudge at checkout can accelerate upgrades.

Impact: Medium–High
REC 4

Silver Activation — First Points Boost

Offer 2x points on the 2nd purchase for Silver members to build momentum. Tie to the $20–$30 spend cohort (48.27% conversion rate — highest in base).

Impact: Medium

2. Retention & Reducing Drop-Off

▼

The single biggest revenue risk for Megabus Canada is the 61% drop-off after the 1st purchase. Retention improves significantly after the 2nd purchase, making the 1st→2nd conversion the highest-priority intervention point.

🚨 61% of members never return after their 1st purchase. Only 89,356 out of 228,588 make a 2nd purchase — this is the #1 retention gap to close.
📉 AOV drops 17.63% from 1st to 2nd purchase. Despite this, repeat buyers contribute 63.7% of revenue — proving that frequency outweighs order size.
✅ Once members reach their 3rd+ purchase, retention improves and average days between purchases begins to decrease — indicating building brand affinity.
💡 27.27% of repeaters make same-day multi-orders — this segment shows strong intent and brand preference. Deserves dedicated recognition and perks.
REC 5

1st → 2nd Purchase Win-Back Journey

Trigger an automated re-engagement sequence at day 30, 49, and 80 post 1st purchase (matching P50, avg, P80 cycle). Offer a time-limited bonus points incentive.

Impact: High
REC 6

Post-Purchase Welcome Program

Immediately after 1st purchase, enroll members in a loyalty welcome series — show tier progress, highlight benefits, and provide a "next trip" discount code.

Impact: High
REC 7

Same-Day Multi-Order Segment — VIP Fast Track

Identify the 24,365 same-day multi-order members. These planned travelers show high affinity — offer bundle perks, priority status, or fast-track tier upgrades.

Impact: Medium–High
REC 8

AOV Protection — Minimum Spend Bonus

Counter the 17.63% AOV drop on 2nd purchase by offering bonus points for orders above the 1st purchase amount. Reward members who maintain or grow spend.

Impact: Medium

🤝 mega_affiliate_ca holds 18.44% of members but only 14.66% of revenue — lower RPM and frequency vs. web. A loyalty bridge for affiliate users could significantly close this gap.

REC 9

Web-First Loyalty Experience

Embed real-time points balance, tier progress bar, and personalized offers directly in the mega_web_ca booking flow — pre, during, and post transaction.

Impact: High
REC 10

Affiliate → Direct Channel Migration

Offer affiliate members a one-time bonus for completing their next booking directly via web. This increases RPM, frequency, and loyalty program visibility.

Impact: Medium–High
REC 11

Agency / Corporate Loyalty Sub-Program

Create a group/corporate tier for mega_agency_ca users. High AOV ($99) suggests business travel — recurring perks, invoice billing, and volume rewards could drive frequency from 1.21 to 2+.

Impact: Medium
REC 12

Cross-Channel Points Unification

Ensure points earned via affiliate and agency channels are fully visible and redeemable on web. Unified balance view drives web login and increases direct engagement.

Impact: Medium

4. Purchase Cycle & Re-Engagement Timing

▼

Understanding when members are most likely to return is critical for well-timed loyalty communications. The data provides clear percentile-based windows for each purchase step.

📅 Average purchase gap is 49 days (1.6 months). 80% of returners come back within 80 days. This defines the optimal re-engagement window.
📈 Purchase gaps decrease with each subsequent order — from 48 days (1→2) down to 24 days (9→10) — confirming that loyalty and habit build over time.
🚍 Coach Canada repeaters cycle every 46 days avg; Badder Bus repeaters every 56 days. Carrier-specific timing can power personalized re-engagement.

REC 13

3-Touch Re-Engagement Cadence

Day 15: "We miss you" soft nudge + points reminder. Day 48: Time-limited offer (2x points or discount). Day 83: Final "don't lose your status" urgency message.

Impact: High
REC 14

Carrier-Specific Timing Triggers

Personalize re-engagement timing based on the member's preferred carrier. Coach Canada members: trigger at 46 days. Badder Bus members: trigger at 56 days.

Impact: Medium–High
REC 15

Seasonal Demand Amplification

July and August are peak months (10.3–10.8% revenue share). Launch "Summer Loyalty Boost" campaigns 3–4 weeks prior to capitalize on high-intent travel periods.

Impact: Medium
REC 16

Thu–Fri High-Intent Targeting

Thursday (14.93%) and Friday (14.81%) are peak booking days. Schedule loyalty communications (points expiry, offers, tier updates) on Wed–Thu for maximum open rates.

Impact: Medium
